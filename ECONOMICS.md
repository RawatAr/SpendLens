# ECONOMICS.md

## Unit Economics of SpendLens

### Value of a Converted Lead
A "converted lead" for Credex is a startup that successfully purchases discounted AI infrastructure credits.
- **Estimated Average Order Value (AOV)**: $5,000/year in credits.
- **Credex Margin**: ~15-20% ($750 - $1,000 per customer).
- **LTV**: Assuming a 3-year retention, a lead is worth **$2,250 - $3,000** in gross profit.

### CAC by Channel
- **Organic Social (X/LinkedIn)**: $0 (Time-intensive).
- **SEO/Content**: $0 (Long-term play).
- **Referrals**: $100 credit perk per successful referral.
- **Paid Search (Targeting "Claude pricing")**: ~$5-10 CPC. At a 5% lead-to-consultation rate, CAC would be **$200 per consultation**.

### Profitability Thresholds
To be profitable, the funnel needs to perform as follows:
- **Audit Completion -> Lead Capture**: 25%
- **Lead Capture -> Consultation Booked**: 10%
- **Consultation -> Credit Purchase**: 20%
- **Overall Conversion (Audit -> Purchase)**: 0.5%

**The Math**: 200 audits = 1 Sale. 
If 200 audits cost $2,000 in paid traffic (worst case), and 1 sale yields $3,000 LTV, the tool is **LTV/CAC positive (1.5x)**. On organic traffic, it is infinitely profitable.

### The Path to $1M ARR
To drive $1M ARR in 18 months, SpendLens would need:
1. **Volume**: ~200 new credit customers (at $5k AOV).
2. **Funnel**: At a 0.5% conversion rate, we need **40,000 completed audits**.
3. **Velocity**: ~2,200 audits per month.
4. **Strategy**: Scale the "Unfair Distribution" by partnering with startup accelerators (YC, Techstars) to offer SpendLens as a standard onboarding tool for their cohorts.
