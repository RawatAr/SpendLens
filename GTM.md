# GTM.md (Go-To-Market Plan)

## Target User Persona
The exact target user is a **Founding Engineer or CTO at a Seed to Series A startup (5-25 headcount)**. 
- **The Pain**: They are the ones putting the "AI tools" on their personal or corporate credit card. 
- **The Trigger**: They just got their monthly AWS/OpenAI bill and realized it's 2x what it was 3 months ago.

## Search & Discovery Behavior
Right before using SpendLens, our user is likely Googling or scrolling through:
- "Cursor business vs pro seats"
- "How to reduce OpenAI API costs"
- "Claude Team plan minimum seats"
- Scrolling through **"What's in your AI stack?"** threads on X/Twitter.

## Online Hangouts
- **Subreddits**: r/startups, r/cscareerquestions (for tool comparisons), r/SaaS.
- **Slack/Discord**: YC Bookface (if accessible), Launch House, South Park Commons Discord.
- **X Lists**: "AI Infrastructure", "Founding Engineers", "Next.js ecosystem".

## First 100 Users ($0 Budget)
1. **The "Wait, Really?" Thread on X**: Post a screenshot of an audit showing $5,000/yr savings from a simple Cursor/Claude tier-flip. Tag the founders of those tools respectfully.
2. **Reddit "Roast My Stack"**: Offer free audits in r/startups for founders who post their monthly tool spend.
3. **Product Hunt "Ship"**: Launch as a free utility. PH is the natural home for "Mint for X" style tools.
4. **Direct Outreach**: DM 20 founders on X per day with a "I built this tool to help startups stop overpaying for AI — thought you might find it useful."

## Unfair Distribution Channel
**Credex's Existing Network**: Credex already sells infrastructure credits. By embedding SpendLens into the Credex homepage and sending it to the existing email list as a "Free Health Check," the tool gets instant high-trust distribution to the exact people who already need it.

## Week-1 Traction Goals
- **1,000 unique visitors**.
- **200 completed audits**.
- **50 captured leads** (25% conversion).
- **5-10 Credex consultation requests** (High-savings cases).
